Thai Grading Portal

Introduction

Thai Grading Portal is a collection of services that help teachers in Thailand's public schools to record, maintain and modify grades for their classes. Grading can be a very complicated process, and with large class sizes, it can also be hard to keep track of student names, grades, attendance, and lesson progress.

TGP provides a way for teachers to share this information among each other indirectly, so teachers can spend less time collecting and managing their classes' information, and more time focusing on quality lessons and one on one interaction with students.

Grading

TGP's core feature is its grade-viewing and -modifying page. Teachers can view their class rosters with simple grade summaries, (in the format of standard Thai public school gradebooks,) easily modify them through TGP's interface, or export them to Excel and Google Sheets.

Attendance

In addition to grade information being stored and modified through TGP, teachers can also take attendance using TGP's interface, or through their preference of Excel or Google Sheets. In the latter case, attendance data can be imported the same as grade data, and most importantly, exported in the proper format as well. If your school's administration is using TGP, they can mark national or school holidays on TGP's calendar to prevent attendance being taken by mistaken on holidays.

Importing and Exporting

As well as exporting grading data from TGP's site, teachers can also import their grade data from .xlsx, .csv and Google Sheets workbooks if they prefer to use TGP's interface. Teachers can swap between different formats as often as they like, and there are advantages to doing so.

For example, if a teacher has been managing their grades locally using Google Sheets, but they need to transfer them to school gradebook format, they can import their Google Sheets workbook, use TGP's interface to finalize their grades, and then export it to their school's gradebook format .xlsx file to be printed and given to administrators.

Student Profiles

There will often be students whose performance doesn't match up with the rest of their class, whether it's better or worse. With student profiles, teachers can get some insight into their student's performance by looking at the student's average performance for the semester across all classes and remarks from other teachers, as well as average performance by field of study. These pieces of information can give teachers a more well-rounded understanding of their students.

Class Profiles

In a similar fashion, whole classes may have widely varying performance in the same subject. Class profiles allow teachers to see in which subject each class shines, as well as their weak points. Teachers who teach the same class will be able to see remarks that other teachers choose to share, as well as their own private remarks on each class. This allows teachers to keep track of which teaching methods are effective with each class.
